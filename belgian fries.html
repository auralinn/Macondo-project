Instructions

First of all, wash and peel the potatoes. Some people rather have it unpeeled, but try them peeled first.

Cut the potatoes lengthways, you can cut them to your preferred thickness.

Thicker potatoes may turn out less crispy. Dry the potatoes, you could do that on some absorbent kitchen towel.

Need more heat!!!! Heat up our oil till it's bubbling. It is prob easier to use a fryer, but you could do this in a pan with a basket. Which I think should be this. But I might be wrong.

Fry the potatoes in batches for about 4 minutes at 125C(Don't worry Americans, I got you. It's 257 degrees Fahrenheit.), but remember not to let the potatoes brown. Remove it from the basket to a plate and let it cool for around 30 minutes(30 minutes quite long, right?). Line the plate with absorbent paper to clean off any oil.

We are going to cook them again after letting them cool. So re-heat the oil and re-fry the potatoes at a 175C(Your on your own Americans. Just kidding, It's 347 degrees Fahrenheit.).

Let the oil heat up to 175C degrees before adding the potatoes and only cooking it for a about a minute till they starting browning. Don't overcook your precious potatoes.

It's finally serving time, I'm hungry. Lightly salt the fries. PLEASE don't over salt your fries, unless you want to, but I don't know why you would do that. I recommend serving it with some mayonnaise. They are special Belgian fries mayonnaise for this dish in Belgium and Holland. It is called fritesaus.
